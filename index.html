Labs for APMA E2000

Here we’ll post activities (“labs” for lack of a better word) for the recitation sections of APMA E2000: Multivariable Calculus. The point is threefold:

  • To revisit fundamental material from this week’s lectures.
  • To introduce some tooling for processing/visualizing content on a deeper level than can typically be done by hand.
  • To bring some creative elements into the activities.

Lab 3 - Circles and Curvature

You may work in groups of up to 3 students. Goals In this activity, you will: Parametrize a circle embedded in $\R^3$. Create curves via different parametrizations. Practice computing curvature. Synthesize the above to plot an osculating circle “by hand.” Circles We know that we can parametrize the unit circle in $\R^2$ via $\langle \cos t, \sin t \rangle$ and adjust the radius and center via dialation and translation: $$\mathbf r(t) = \langle R\cos(t) + h, R\sin(t) + k \rangle$$ But what about embedding a circle in $\R^3$?
